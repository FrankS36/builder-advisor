How it works

Three components, one interaction pattern

01

You bring a decision

"I'm thinking about charging $49/mo for my design tool. My competitors charge $15. Am I crazy?"

02

The system finds relevant patterns

Your agent queries the MCP server, which matches your situation against a library of decision patterns drawn from real solo builder journeys. It pulls challenge questions, common mistakes, and anti-patterns.

03

Your agent challenges you

Instead of saying "Great idea!" or "That's too high," your agent asks the specific hard questions this situation demands. It might reference your own past pricing decisions and their outcomes.

04

You decide, and the system remembers

Your decision and reasoning get logged. Next time you face a similar choice, the system has precedent — a mirror that shows you your own patterns.

What it looks like in practice

A real interaction

I've been working on this SaaS for 4 months. It works, but I keep thinking I need to add team collaboration features before I launch. My competitor has it and users will expect it.

Let me push back on this a bit.

You said it "works." That means someone can get value from it today. You've been building for 4 months. This is the third feature you've described as a prerequisite for launch this conversation.

This looks like The One More Thing Loop. Here's what I want you to sit with:

Your competitor built team features after they had paying users. You're trying to build them before you have any. Why are you solving a scaling problem when you haven't validated the core problem?

Concrete question: If you showed this to a potential customer today — no team features — what's the worst thing that could happen?

If the answer is "they'd say it's useful but they need team features," that's a buying signal, not a reason to delay. You just learned what to build next AND that they want your product.

What would you need to see to feel comfortable launching this week?

Get started in 60 seconds

One file. No server required.

01

Download the skill file

One markdown file is all you need. It works standalone — no MCP server, no dependencies.

curl -O https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FrankS36/builder-advisor/main/skill/SKILL.md
02

Drop it in your project

Put it where your agent can find it. For Claude Code, that's the skills directory.

mkdir -p .claude/skills && mv SKILL.md .claude/skills/builder-advisor.md
03

Ask your agent about any decision

That's it. Next time you mention a decision — pricing, launching, pivoting — your agent will challenge your thinking instead of rubber-stamping it.

"I'm thinking about charging $49/mo but my competitors charge $15. What do you think?"

Want persistence and decision journaling? Set up the MCP server for the full experience.
